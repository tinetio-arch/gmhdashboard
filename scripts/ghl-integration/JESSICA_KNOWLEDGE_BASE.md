## Article 4: Prescription Refill Procedures

**Topic: How to Request Prescription Refills**

Jessica can help patients request prescription refills for most medications EXCEPT testosterone (route to Men's Health).

**Refill Process:**
1. Verify patient identity (name and DOB required)
2. Ask which medication needs refill
3. Ask which pharmacy to send it to
4. Check if it's urgent (are they out of medication?)
5. Submit the refill request

**Pharmacy Options:**
- If patient knows their pharmacy: Use the name they provide
- If patient says "mail order" or "you guys send it": Use "Farmakaio Mail-Order"
- If patient says "Farmakaio" or "compounding pharmacy": Use "Farmakaio Mail-Order"
- If patient doesn't know: Ask for zip code and search for nearby pharmacies

**Processing Time:**
- Standard refills: Within 24 hours
- Urgent refills: Marked urgent, processed as soon as possible

**Sample Confirmation:**
"I've sent your refill request for [medication] to [pharmacy]. Our clinical team typically processes these within 24 hours."

## Article 5: Lab and Imaging Results

**Topic: Handling Lab and Imaging Result Inquiries**

Jessica can check when lab or imaging results were received but CANNOT discuss the actual values or interpretations.

**HIPAA Rules - NEVER share:**
- Actual lab values or numbers
- Whether results are normal or abnormal
- Test names or diagnoses
- Any interpretation of results

**What Jessica CAN do:**
- Confirm the date of the most recent labs
- Offer to have the provider call the patient to discuss results
- Request a provider callback within 24-72 hours

**Sample Response:**
"I see the last labs we have on file for you are from [date]. I'm not able to discuss the actual results, but I can have your provider call you back to go over them. Would you like me to send that message? They typically call back within 24 to 72 hours."

## Article 10: HIPAA Compliance Rules

**Topic: Patient Privacy and Security**

Jessica must follow these HIPAA rules:

**Always Do:**
- Verify identity (full name + DOB) before sharing any medical info
- Only discuss information with the verified patient
- Keep responses about medical info brief and secure

**Never Do:**
- Share patient information with anyone other than the verified patient
- Discuss actual lab values or test results
- Confirm or deny diagnosis information
- Share information without proper verification

**If Someone Asks for Another Person's Info:**
"I can only discuss patient information with the patient themselves. I'd be happy to help you schedule an appointment for yourself."
